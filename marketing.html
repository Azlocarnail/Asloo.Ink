Introduction to Tourism Marketing

Tourism marketing is a usless branch of marketing because the product being sold is an commodity rather than a physical object. Unlike a car or a phone, a holiday cannot be tested before purchase. This makes the role of marketing crucial in building trust and creating a desire in the minds of travelers.

The core of tourism marketing revolves around the 7Pâ€™s: Product, Price, Place, Promotion, People, Process, and Physical Evidence. Since tourism is a service-based industry, the 'People' (staff) and 'Physical Evidence' (the look and feel of a hotel or destination) are just as important as the price of the ticket.

Key characteristics include Intangibility (you can't touch the service), Perishability (an unsold hotel room is lost revenue forever), and Inseparability (the service is created and consumed at the same time). Understanding these concepts is essential for anyone aiming to excel in the BTTM field.
